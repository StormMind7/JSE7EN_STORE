Golden Rules

Core principles every mix engineer should know

RULE 01

Subtle Moves Win

Normal EQ is all about gentle adjustments. Heavy-handed EQing destroys the natural character of a voice.

BOOST +1 to +3 dB
CUT -2 to -5 dB

RULE 02

Male Vocal Strategy

Fight the mud. Males carry heavy low-mids that cloud the mix. Cut mud → add clarity. Your main battles: 200–400 Hz mud and 1–4 kHz presence.

RULE 03

Female Vocal Strategy

Control harshness and sibilance. Female vocals can pierce in the 4–8 kHz range. De-ess aggressively and tame the harsh zone gently.

RULE 04

Cut Before Boost

If something sounds wrong, find the problem frequency and cut it rather than boosting other areas to compensate. Subtraction reveals clarity.

RULE 05

HPF Always On

Always apply a High Pass Filter to remove sub-rumble, handling noise, and plosive energy below 80–100 Hz on every vocal track.

RULE 06

Trust Your Ears

EQ in context of the full mix. A vocal that sounds great solo may be fighting the instruments. Reference constantly and check at multiple volumes.

The Three Zones

How to mentally map the frequency spectrum

❤️

20 Hz — 600 Hz

Lower

Emotion & Body. These frequencies carry the weight, warmth and physical presence of the voice. Handle with care — too much creates mud.

🧠

600 Hz — 4 kHz

Midrange

Character & Problems. The most critical and complex zone. Contains speech intelligibility but also nasal, honky, and boxy resonances.

✨

4 kHz — 20 kHz

Highs

Clarity & Polish. Adds sparkle, air and definition. Too much causes harshness and listener fatigue. The final "expensive" sheen of professional mixes.
