01 — Introduction

What Is SSL G Channel?

The SSL G Channel is a plugin emulation of the legendary SSL 4000 G console's channel strip — used by world-class mixing engineers on every genre from Pop to Orchestral.

🎛️
Console-in-a-Plugin
Packs Filters, a 4-band EQ, Compressor, Expander/Gate, and Routing into one powerful strip — like a mini mixing console.
🎚️
Mix Bus "Glue"
Its compressor is famous for glueing individual tracks into a cohesive, polished mix without squashing transients.
🎵
Gentle Shaping
Think 0.5–2 dB moves, not heavy surgery. SSL is a refiner, not a fixer. For big problems, reach for Pro-Q3.
🏆
Industry Standard
Used on records by Michael Jackson, Nirvana, Daft Punk, and virtually every major release since the 1980s.

02 — Why & When

Why Use It & When

Understanding when to reach for SSL vs. other tools is half the battle. Here's the clear decision framework.

✅
Use SSL G When…
• Your mix sounds "unglued" or loose
• You need subtle tonal polish (+/- 1–2 dB)
• Bass feels muddy or undefined
• Mix sounds flat and lifeless (Analog ON)
• On the Master or Mix Bus (2-bus)
❌
Don't Use SSL When…
• A specific frequency needs heavy correction (use Pro-Q3)
• Multiband compression is needed (use Pro-MB)
• You need surgical narrow-Q cuts
• Heavy limiting is required (use a limiter)
• Trying to fix a bad mix at stem level
🔗
Typical Signal Chain
SSL G → (optional) Pro-Q3 → Limiter

SSL comes first — it shapes and glues before you hit the limiter.

07
🔘 ROUTING — EQ TO & DYN TO
Routing Brain
▼
EQ TO — Sidechain Routing
Tells the compressor's sidechain what signal to listen to. FLT DYN S.C means the filtered signal feeds the sidechain, preventing bass from over-triggering the compressor.
BYP — Bypassed FLT DYN S.C ✅ RECOMMENDED
DYN TO — Dynamics Output
Where the dynamics section sends its signal. BYP routes straight to channel output without extra routing.
BYP ✅ RECOMMENDED CH OUT
💡 Why FLT DYN S.C? Without sidechain filtering, a heavy kick drum hit can constantly over-trigger the compressor on the mix bus — causing pumping on every beat. FLT DYN S.C filters the sidechain so the compressor responds to the full mix, not just bass hits.
08

Bottom Switch
▼
Analog ON/OFF
Engages a subtle harmonic saturation and noise floor emulation. Adds the "vibe" and warmth that makes digital mixes feel analog. Barely audible but deeply felt — like the difference between LED and incandescent light.
ON = Subtle analog color ✅ OFF = Clean/digital
✓ Always ON for mix bus work

06 — Metering & Verification

Verify With SPAN

Use Voxengo SPAN (free) after SSL G to confirm your settings are working correctly. Here's what to look for.

Zone 1 — Low End (20–300 Hz)
Smooth Sub & Bass
Should be a clean, descending slope. No bumps or peaks sticking out at 200–300 Hz.
✓ Good: Smooth slope, no mud bumps
❌ Bad: Spike at 200–400 Hz = too much LMF
Zone 2 — Mids (1–5 kHz)
Controlled Mid Range
Should not be spiky or aggressive. A slight dip at 2–4 kHz is normal and healthy.
✓ Good: Gentle, not spiky or harsh
❌ Bad: Big peak at 3 kHz = too much HMF gain
Zone 3 — Highs (8–20 kHz)
Natural Air Roll-off
Should gently roll off above 10 kHz. A slight shelf is natural. Hard spike = too much HF boost.
✓ Good: Smooth roll-off after 10 kHz
❌ Bad: Sharp spike = too much HF
Stereo Correlation
Phase Health Check
The correlation meter shows how the left and right channels relate. Positive = healthy stereo. Negative = phase problems.
✓ Good: +0.2 to +1.0
❌ Bad: 0 or negative = phase issues

07 — Common Mistakes

What NOT To Do

Avoid these and you'll immediately sound more professional.

❌
Boosting more than 2 dB — SSL is for gentle shaping. Anything over 2 dB will sound unnatural on a mix bus.
❌
Using Gate on master — Gate/Expander is for individual tracks with noise issues. On the mix bus it will cause choppy, broken audio.
❌
Over-compressing (5–6 dB GR) — This kills the life of your mix. Keep gain reduction to 1–2 dB maximum.
❌
Boosting at 3 kHz — This is the harshness zone. Even a +1 dB boost here causes ear fatigue and listener drop-off.
❌
Not using EQ TO: FLT DYN S.C — Without sidechain filtering, your kick drum will pump-trigger the compressor every beat.
❌
Using SSL as a fix-all — If something needs 4+ dB of correction, fix it in the individual tracks first. SSL is a polisher, not a surgeon.

🧠 The SSL Mindset

Think of SSL G like seasoning food — a pinch of salt enhances the dish, a handful ruins it. Every move should be subtle, intentional, and in service of the overall feel. If you find yourself making big corrections, step back and fix the mix at the source first.
